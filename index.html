Pretotyping

What is pretotyping?

Pretotyping is a way of using creative, manual experiments to get YODA (your own data) to validate your startup idea.

Why is pretotyping so important?

The business failure rate is over 90% and the majority of the time it's because aspiring entrepreneurs pursue the wrong business idea. Pretotyping solves this by catching wrong ideas early to save time and money.

Couldn't I just use surveys to validate my idea?

Surveys aren't always the most effective method because without skin in the game, statements aren't always consistent with action. A person could answer "yes, I would buy this product" on a survey, but when the actual product comes out, he/she wouldn't even give the order page a look. Skin in the game (where the user has to put something at risk, usually money or time) is a better true measurement of consumer demand.

How is pretotyping different to prototyping?

Prototyping asks the question, "can we build it" while pretotyping asks the questions, "should we build it." You can use prototypes as a pretotype if you'd like, but if you are not an extremely proficient designer, there are faster, cheaper and more effective pretotype methods to validate your idea.

How does pretotyping relate to Lean Startup?

Pretotyping corresponds very well with Lean Startup methodology, and we at Nifty are big fans of Lean Startup thinking! That being said, we like to think of pretotyping as even leaner than Lean Startup and even more minimal than MVP.

The Methods

Mechanical Turk

Using a human to substitute for computer algorithm

Pinocchio

A fake physical product that doesn't work but makes for good simulation

Fake Door

Landing pages or fake door signs

Facade

A form of fake door where a basic interaction happens/customer actually gets something

YouTube

Making a video about the hypothetical product/service, then seeing how many would commit over long term

One Night Stand

A short term commitment to a new product/service

Infiltrator

Sneaking a product into someone else's store to see if people are interested

Relabel

Take an existing product, and relabel it with your own label to see what the interest is

Live Demo

Demonstration in front of live audience

Measuring 'skin in the game'

We assign points to different forms of engagement to more systematically measure engagement

Views, likes, poll results and opinions:

0 points. They're not representative of user actions.

Time committment

1 point per minute

Cash deposit for waiting list

1 point per £1
